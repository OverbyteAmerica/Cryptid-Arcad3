FULTON COUNTY

Incident Overview

In early 2024, Fulton County, Georgia suffered a ransomware attack that forced multiple court systems, public services, and internal operations offline. The incident revealed weaknesses in endpoint protection, credential hygiene, and incident response readiness.

CIA Triad Impact

Confidentiality
Potential exposure of sensitive legal and employee records.
Integrity
System trust degraded due to encrypted and unavailable records.
Availability
Court operations and county services disrupted for weeks.

Attack Path Visualization

The attack likely began with a compromised endpoint, followed by lateral movement through internal systems and eventual deployment of ransomware across critical services.

MITRE ATT&CK Mapping

Initial Access Phishing or Valid Account Abuse (T1566 / T1078)
Lateral Movement Remote Services & Credential Reuse (T1021)
Impact Data Encrypted for Impact (T1486)

Recommended Remediations

Endpoint Hardening Deploy EDR, restrict admin privileges, and enforce MFA across endpoints.
Network Segmentation Limit lateral movement between departments and critical services.
Incident Response Readiness Maintain offline backups, tabletop exercises, and rapid containment playbooks.
